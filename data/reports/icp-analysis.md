# ICP & Segmentation Analysis Report

**Period:** March 20, 2025 – December 20, 2025
**Deals Analyzed:** 23 closed-won deals (out of 40 total; 57.5% overall win rate)
**Total Won ACV:** $2,043,000 | **Total Lost ACV:** $1,691,000
**Report Generated:** 2026-03-17

---

## Executive Summary

The data from 40 closed deals reveals a highly differentiated ICP: the best customers are mid-to-large enterprises (2,500–6,200 employees, $500M–$2B+ annual revenue) in Financial Services, Technology, Life Sciences, and Insurance/Healthcare, where the win rate is 88–100% and average won ACV is $105K–$195K. The single strongest predictor of a won deal is whether the economic buyer was engaged — 23 of 23 wins with an engaged economic buyer versus 0 wins without one, a binary signal that should immediately change how deals are qualified. Partner referrals and conference leads are the highest-ROI channels, each producing an 83% win rate and $117K–$119K average ACV, compared to outbound SDR at 30% win rate despite producing the highest individual ACVs. The company should shift territory prioritization toward Financial Services, Technology, and Life Sciences in the 2,500–5,500 employee band, downgrade outbound SDR volume in Defense, Logistics, and Manufacturing (0% win rate across 8 total deals), and require economic buyer contact as a mandatory qualification gate before any deal advances past discovery.

## Warning Segments

### Warning 1: Outbound SDR into Enterprise — High Volume, Low Conversion, Structural Mismatch

**Evidence:** Outbound SDR produced 10 deals but only 3 wins (30% WR). Of the 7 losses: D005 Atlas Manufacturing ($210K, 219 days, budget reallocated), D015 Ironclad Defense ($250K, 270 days, FedRAMP blocker), D028 Titan Aerospace ($220K, 263 days, procurement killed deal), D036 Beacon Telecom ($140K, 177 days, Salesforce bundle). These 4 deals alone consumed an estimated 929 rep-days with zero revenue return. The wins via Outbound (D010 Vanguard, D021 Emerald, D001 Nextera) all had MEDDIC >= 9, compliance/mandate urgency, and early EB engagement — indicating the channel works only when qualification is rigorous upfront.

**Action:** Restrict Outbound SDR sequencing to accounts that match Tier 1 firmographic profile AND have known RevOps compliance or forecast-miss pain signals. Remove Defense, Manufacturing, Logistics, and Telecom from SDR targeting lists immediately.

### Warning 2: Healthcare — Promising ACV but Structural Budget Risk

**Evidence:** Healthcare is 2W/2L (50%) with avg won ACV $89,500 — attractive on paper. However, both losses (D003 Orion Health Systems, $165K lost to Salesforce; D013 Summit Health Partners, $180K lost to Gong) failed on the same pattern: champion without CFO/CIO access. D013 consumed 229 days with a VP Sales champion who tried to schedule CFO 3 times and failed. D003's IT director actively blocked the deal. Healthcare healthcare wins (D030 Crimson Medical, D037 Coral Clinic) both had either direct EB engagement or partner-warm-intro bypassing procurement friction. Total ACV lost in Healthcare: $345,000 across two deals.

**Action:** Gate Healthcare deals on: (1) compliance mandate explicitly stated in discovery, (2) economic buyer (CFO or CIO) meeting secured within 45 days, (3) no IT director identified as primary stakeholder without executive override.

### Warning 3: Webinar Lead Source — Deceptive Volume, Below-Average Conversion

**Evidence:** Webinar Lead produced 6 deals but only 2 wins (33% WR). The 4 losses: D011 Horizon Media ($48K, champion left), D022 Pacific Retail ($40K, lost to Gong), D034 Falcon Logistics ($36K, lost to Clari), D039 Evergreen Education ($32K, budget cut). Average lost ACV from webinar: $39K. The 2 wins (D006 Crestview Financial, $55K; D029 Maple Leaf Finance, $62K) were both Mid-Market Financial Services with compliance urgency — suggesting webinar content self-selects for less-qualified buyers outside the ICP. Webinar leads consume discovery and demo cycles that could be applied to higher-intent inbound or referral leads.

**Action:** Qualify webinar leads against ICP firmographic filter before assigning to AE. Add immediate scoring on industry and company size — disqualify Logistics, Education, Media before first AE contact. Route Financial Services webinar leads to immediate follow-up.

### Warning 4: SMB Segment — High Win Rate Masking Post-Sale Risk

**Evidence:** SMB has the highest win rate (71%, 5/7) but the worst post-sale outcomes. Four of five SMB accounts are at risk or borderline: Granite Builders (Health 60, NPS 55, 3 open tickets, 10 in 90 days), Neon Digital (Health 62, NPS 60, 2 open tickets, 7 in 90 days), Quantum Dynamics (Health 65, NPS 58, 2 open tickets, 8 in 90 days), Coral Clinic (Health 68, NPS 63). The single healthy SMB account is Meridian Consulting (Health 70, NPS 62 — barely above threshold). The $19,800 average ACV and high churn risk means SMB likely has negative LTV impact after CS cost is factored in.

**Action:** Conduct an immediate churn risk review on Granite Builders, Neon Digital, and Quantum Dynamics. Set a minimum ACV of $22K for SMB deals going forward (equivalent to Meridian Consulting, the only healthy SMB). Consider a product-led or self-serve motion for sub-$20K, removing rep time allocation.

### Warning 5: Single-Threaded Enterprise Deals — Champion Fragility Risk

**Evidence:** Three lost Enterprise deals failed due to single-threading: D032 Cobalt Mining ($175K, 204 days — VP Sales fired, new VP chose Gong), D028 Titan Aerospace ($220K, 263 days — VP Sales lost internal momentum, procurement killed deal), D011 Horizon Media ($48K — champion left company). In all three cases, the deal was built on a single contact with no backup EB or evaluator engaged. Contrast with won deals D018 Northstar (2 logged contacts, CRO was champion AND EB), D001 Nextera (3 contacts: VP Sales champion + CFO + Head of RevOps), D008 Pinnacle (3 contacts: CRO + VP Sales champion + IT Director).

**Action:** Require minimum two confirmed contacts (champion + EB or evaluator) in CRM before any Enterprise deal advances past Stage 2. Flag any Enterprise deal with only one contact as "single-threaded risk" and assign SDR to expand coverage within 30 days.

## Gap Analysis vs Current Targeting

Based on the deal data, the following gaps exist between what is being pursued and what the ICP evidence supports:

**Gap 1 — Defense and Government Contractor Targeting Without FedRAMP**
The pipeline includes D015 Ironclad ($250K) and D028 Titan Aerospace ($220K), both consuming 260+ days each with zero chance of closing due to a structural product gap (no FedRAMP). These two deals alone consumed ~530 rep-days. If there are any similar deals in the current open pipeline, they should be immediately disqualified. Total wasted effort on these two deals: $470K in lost ACV and approximately 7.5 rep-months of selling time.

**Gap 2 — Outbound SDR Targeting Low-Win-Rate Industries**
Of the 10 outbound-sourced deals, 7 are in low- or zero-WR industries (Manufacturing, Defense, Logistics, Telecom, Construction). Only 3 outbound deals were in Tier 1 industries (Vanguard Insurance, Nextera Corp, Emerald Technologies) — and all three won. This implies SDR lists are not filtered against the ICP, leading to a structural misallocation of outbound sequences.

**Gap 3 — Financial Services Underpenetrated via Outbound**
Financial Services is the highest win rate industry (100%, 4/4 wins) with an average ACV of $105K and average cycle of 125 days. However, only 1 of the 4 Financial Services deals came from Outbound SDR (D001 Nextera). Two came from Partner Referral (D018 Northstar, D012 Cascade Energy) and one from Webinar (D029 Maple Leaf). Given the 100% win rate, Financial Services should be the primary target for both Partner development (expanding referral networks in FSI) and targeted conference attendance (FSI-specific events).

**Gap 4 — EMEA Underpenetrated**
EMEA has an 83% win rate across 6 deals ($95.2K avg ACV) — nearly matching the best-performing channels — but represents only 15% of total deal volume (6/40). The EMEA wins include D023 Zenith Pharma ($190K), D008 Pinnacle Retail ($145K), D019 Apex Biotech ($58K), D035 Aurora Software ($65K), D004 Quantum Dynamics ($18K). The single EMEA loss was D022 Pacific Retail (APAC, not EMEA proper). Expanding EMEA coverage — particularly in Financial Services, Technology, and Life Sciences — should be a territory investment priority.

**Gap 5 — Economic Buyer Engagement Not a Hard Gate**
Of the 15 deals where the economic buyer was never met, 9 are in the CRM with enough activity to indicate the deal was actively worked for 45–229 days before dying. This represents a qualification process failure — the current process is advancing deals without requiring EB access, consuming significant rep capacity with a mathematically zero win rate.

## Recommended Actions

1. **Implement an Economic Buyer Gate in the Sales Process.** Add "Economic buyer meeting confirmed" as a required field before any deal can advance from Stage 2 (Discovery) to Stage 3 (Technical Evaluation). Any deal open more than 45 days without a confirmed EB meeting should be flagged red in the CRM and escalated to the rep's manager. Based on the data, enforcing this gate alone would have eliminated 15 deals consuming 1,800+ combined rep-days that had a 0% win rate.

2. **Rebuild SDR Target Account Lists Using ICP Firmographic Filters.** Remove Defense, Logistics, Manufacturing, Telecommunications, Mining, Media, and Education from all active SDR outbound sequences. Replace with companies meeting: Financial Services OR Technology OR Life Sciences, 2,000–6,000 employees, annual revenue $500M–$2B, and known RevOps/Salesforce user (proxy: LinkedIn job postings for VP Sales or Head of RevOps). This directly addresses Gap 2 above and is actionable within one sprint.

3. **Invest in Partner Channel Expansion, Specifically in Financial Services and Healthcare.** Partner Referral is tied for the highest win rate (83%) and produces $119K avg ACV. Current wins via partner (D018 Northstar, D023 Zenith, D030 Crimson Medical, D012 Cascade, D037 Coral Clinic) span Financial Services, Life Sciences, Healthcare, and Energy. Build a formal partner program with 3–5 FSI-focused SI partners and HubSpot/Salesforce implementation partners who serve the 500–5,000 employee segment. Target 8 partner-sourced deals in H1 2026 (vs. 5 in the full 2025 dataset).

4. **Update Lead Scoring to Weight Industry and Economic Buyer Access.** Current scoring likely weights engagement signals (email opens, demo requests). Revise: add 30 points for Financial Services, Technology, or Life Sciences industry; add 20 points for 2,000+ employees; add 20 points for $500M+ annual revenue; deduct 30 points for Defense, Logistics, or Manufacturing; add 40 points for any contact record with title "CRO," "CFO," or "CEO." This aligns lead scoring with the binary predictors identified above.

5. **Flag and Address Four At-Risk SMB Accounts Before Next Renewal Cycle.** Granite Builders (renewal Oct 2026, Health 60), Neon Digital (renewal Sep 2026, Health 62), Quantum Dynamics (renewal Sep 2026, Health 65), and Coral Clinic (renewal Oct 2026, Health 68) are all below the 70 health threshold and show elevated support ticket rates. Assign CS to each account with a 90-day improvement plan. If health does not improve to 75+ within 90 days, begin a managed offboarding conversation rather than renewing into churn. Set a forward policy: no new SMB contracts below $22K ACV or below 150 employees.
